01

What Momentum+ is

A subscription, members-only digital community and learning platform from the organizers of TSLS. Premium but grounded — an executive club, not a tech startup.

Momentum+ extends the Summit into a year-round membership: live expert-led sessions, a searchable library of recorded content with AI-generated takeaways, a private tier-gated community, curated partner resources, and direct access to the speakers and collaborators who make TSLS what it is.

The name is the positioning. Attendees leave the Summit with momentum — Momentum+ is how they keep it. The brand must always communicate five things at once:

Continuity

The Summit continues here — the same energy, all year.

Belonging

A community of peers, not anonymous content.

Regional pride

Proudly Tri-State. Rooted in MD, PA & WV.

Substance

Practical and real — never motivational fluff.

Premium value

Worth joining. Worth renewing.

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space equal to the height of the "+" mark on all sides of any lockup. Minimum widths: primary lockup 180 px / 1.6 in; "+" monogram 24 px / 0.25 in. Below these, legibility and the serif detailing break down.

Construction

Wordmark set in Playfair Display SemiBold, tracking −1. The "+" mark's height equals the cap height of "Momentum"; it is optically centered on the cap line and never touches the "m". On dark backgrounds the "+" and reversed wordmark use the brighter gold (#C9A96A) for contrast.

Do & don't

DoUse approved lockups at full opacity on navy, cream, white, or an unbusy photo with a dark overlay. Give the mark room to breathe. Let the gold "+" lead on its own where space is tight.
Don'tRecolor the "+", stretch or condense the wordmark, add drop shadows or gradients, swap the serif for another font, place on a busy background, or set "Momentum+" in a system font as running text.

05

Graphic language

Angles and lines — not icons and emoji. The system is built from thin gold rules, diagonal accents, corner brackets, and geometric shapes. Restraint is the point.

Thin gold rules

Hairline dividers and underlines that frame headlines and separate content with quiet precision.

Diagonal accents

A rising line — momentum as direction. Used sparingly to add motion to covers and section breaks.

Corner brackets

Opposing corner details frame a space — the "executive club" cue that recurs in the app icon and covers.

06

Brand voice

Confident, plain-spoken, and warm. We sound like a respected peer across the table — not a hype-man, not a corporate memo. Politics stay at the door.

Member-facing

Sound like

  • Direct and practical — "here's what you can use Monday morning."
  • Grounded and regional — proud of the Tri-State without being provincial.
  • Inviting and peer-to-peer — a community, not an audience.
  • Quietly premium — the value is obvious, so we don't oversell it.

Avoid

  • Motivational-poster language, hustle-culture, exclamation stacks.
  • Big-city or Silicon-Valley jargon and buzzwords.
  • Emoji and icon-speak in brand copy.
  • Partisan framing of any kind — check politics at the door.

Sponsor-facing

Sound like

  • Credible and specific — real members, real regional reach, real engagement.
  • Respectful of the member experience — sponsorship is tasteful and integrated.
  • Partnership-minded — aligned with a trusted local institution (TSLS + NewsTalk 103.7).

Avoid

  • Overpromising audience size or "impressions" language.
  • Anything that positions the member as a product to be sold.
  • Interruptive, loud ad framing that breaks the premium tone.
